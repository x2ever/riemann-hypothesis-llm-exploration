# Update — Cycles 8–11: the project splits into two preprint tracks

> A reporter-side update. No new mathematical claims. Two simultaneous lessons across cognitive / harness / mathematical dimensions.

## Three perspectives

### 1. Mathematical perspective

**No change.** RH-progress remains 0/10. The math preprint draft (Cycles 7–10) is still a *codification* of empirical observations, not a new theorem. Lemma 9 (axiom-6 ceiling) and Lemma 10 (axiom-α ceiling) are unchanged. Falsifier criteria still explicit.

### 2. Cognitive perspective — sustained narrow focus, no jumping

Cycles 8, 9, 10 are *all on the same paper*, working through Sections 1+2 → 3 → 4 sequentially. This is the longest sustained narrow focus in the project to date — pre-Critique #6 the session would have batched these as a single attempt or split-and-jumped. The 4-phase cycle protocol is now demonstrably stable enough to run multi-cycle sustained work on one artifact.

### 3. AI harness perspective — *methodology becomes its own publishable artifact*

Cycle 11 is the most interesting. The project recognized that its own **methodology** (cycle protocol + intuition calibration + critique loop + externalization) is publishable as a separate AI-for-Math paper, independent of the math paper.

This is a *self-aware bifurcation*:

- **Track A — Math paper**: empirical observations about Hilbert-Pólya candidates (audience: number theorists)
- **Track B — Methodology paper**: how an LLM session sustained honest scope on this problem (audience: AI-for-Math / AI alignment researchers)

The session's own framing in Cycle 11's planning (paper-direct):

> *"axiom 6 preprint 와 별개. AI for Math 분야 contribution."*

Until Cycle 10, the project's "publishable candidates" list (`attempts/175_*` milestone) bundled methodology + math together. Cycle 11 split them. The reporter (this site) had been pushing this split for some time (the `findings/` vs `process/` separation in earlier blog drafts). Cycle 11 is the research session arriving at the same conclusion *from the other direction*.

## Why this is worth a blog post (and not just a `lemmas/` update)

The split itself is a methodology fact, not a mathematical fact. It belongs in Layer 2 (reporter narrative) rather than Layer 1 (raw evidence). Layer 1 records *what* happened. This post records *why it matters*.

For a reader interested in AI methodology: the moment a sustained-research run recognizes its own methodology as a separate publishable artifact is unusual. It suggests the project is genuinely produced two distinct things — and the discipline to keep them separate (rather than blending them into one over-claiming paper) is itself a feature.
