## Introduction Section (publishable draft)

### Title
*"A Catalog of Failed RH Proofs: A 6-Category Framework for Critical Reading"*

### Abstract (final draft)

The Riemann Hypothesis (RH) — first stated by Bernhard Riemann in 1859 [Riemann1859] — has resisted proof for 167 years. Riemann himself acknowledged "fleeting futile attempts" (paper-direct page 4). Edward Bombieri's 1998 Clay Mathematics Institute problem statement [Bombieri1998] notes "the number of failed attempts on a proof" as evidence of the problem's depth.

This paper presents a 6-category framework for systematic critical reading of proposed RH proofs, derived from analysis of historical failures (Riemann 1859 self-acknowledged, Rademacher 1940s, Atiyah 2018, de Branges 1986+, BBM 2017 hypothetical proposals, Lagarias §8 (1) hypothetical):

- (A) Trivial Circular: spectrum identification trivially equivalent to zero condition.
- (B) Reference Circular: core construction depends on unverified external paper.
- (C) Identity Transplant: limited identity used in unrestricted form.
- (D) Multi-valued Inversion: inverse step ignores multi-value.
- (E) Self-acknowledged Speculation: paper acknowledges undecidability while claiming proof.
- (F) Abstraction-Concrete Gap: abstract framework + concrete RH application missing exact step.

Each category is illustrated with paper-direct quotes from primary sources. Application protocols and an audit table for current candidates are provided.

### §1 Introduction: 167 years of attempts

Riemann's 1859 quote (Wilkins translation):
> "very probable that all roots are real. Certainly one would wish for a stricter proof here; I have meanwhile temporarily put aside the search for this after some fleeting futile attempts."

Bombieri (1998 Clay):
> "Notwithstanding some skepticism voiced in the past, based perhaps more on the number of failed attempts on a proof rather than on solid heuristics."

Conrey (2003 Notices):
> "Hans Rademacher, best known for his exact formula for the number of partitions of an integer, thought he had disproved RH... The mathematics community tried to get Time magazine interested in the story... it transpired that Time became interested and published an article only after it was discovered that Rademacher's proof was incorrect."

→ paper-direct: 167 years multiple failed attempts.
